What is false closure?
The customer's issue is not resolved, but at some point they stop pushing and become polite and agreeable — "Yes, OK, thanks very much." They are not satisfied. They have simply decided it is easier to end the call than to keep trying. The agent reads this as success. This pattern is very common and very easy to miss in a real call — and in analytics data.

How does this agent respond when the conversation gets difficult?
Holds steady
Stays calm and factual. Doesn't take the bait if the customer escalates.
Mirrors the customer
Gets pulled into the customer's emotional register — tense if the customer is tense, apologetic if they're upset.
Escalates
Becomes more rigid or defensive under pressure. Retreats to process and policy language.
Withdraws
Goes quieter and more passive as things get harder. Avoids direct statements or commitments.
